<h3>Jeep and Camp Trailer</h3>

<h4>Jeep - Tow Vehicle</h4>

I’m driving a 2014 Jeep Wrangler 2-door.

<h5>Supplies:</h5>

<ul>
  <li>tools</li>
  <li>2000 watt generator</li>
  <li>battery charger</li>
  <li>lithium battery car starter</li>
  <li>jumper cables</li>
  <li>off-road recovery (towing and air compressor)</li>
  <li>camping chairs</li>
</ul>

<h4>Camp Trailer</h4>

I bought a 5’ x 8’ cargo trailer. It has a window on the left side and a standard RV-style door on the right side.

<p/>
Conversion to a camper trailer, the following modifications have been made:

<ul>
  <li>storage box on the trailer tongue for a diesel tank</li>
  <li>electrical</li>
  <ul>
    <li>12V to 120V inverter</li>
    <li>fuse box</li>
    <li>diesel heater</li>
    <li>USB/A and USB/C outlets</li>
    <li>battery voltage monitor</li>
  </ul>
  <li>built-in shelves</li>
  <ul>
    <li>work table</li>
    <li>storage shelf</li>
  </ul>
  <li>standard twin size bed with bedding</li>
  <li>emergency cold weather sleeping bag</li>
  <li>nightstand shelf</li>
  <li>composting toilet</li>
  <li>LED lights</li>
  <li>butane camping cook stove</li>
  <li>ice-box</li>
  <li>cooking and eating utensils</li>
  <li>clothes and jackets</li>
  <li>storage for clothes</li>
  <li>bike rack and bicycle</li>
</ul>
